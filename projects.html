is a MOOC - Massive Open Online Course - Platform started by MIT and Harvard to make college education from top universities such as MIT, Harvard, and Berkeley free and accessible to all through video lectures and homework problems assigned and graded online. I worked in the research division, using data collected from edx users to identify patterns and analyze behavior.

previously the lab discovered a cheating phenomenon that only happens in MOOC environments which they coined CAMEO [Copying Answers Using Multiple Existences Online]. CAMEO involved students creating more than one account to attempt homework and exam problems, harvesting correct answers to input into their primary account.

as a researcher in the lab, I worked on pursuing an interesting theory. A PhD student in the lab discovered that although most CAMEO users scored high on CAMEO-able questions and low on non-CAMEO-able questions, the highest scoring users on both parameters were also CAMEO users. This led to the question: do certain approaches to "cheating" in online learning environments lead to higher learning gains?

thus began my many weeks of data analysis, using Google Bigquery, STATA and Gephi to visualize and identify patterns in student habits and their associated content mastery levels.

is the parent social benefit organization for 1Room. 1Room [formally known as RARE: Radically Affordable Rural-Engineered Education] aims to make education more accessible, affordable, and effective in developing countries. Our pilot sites include Mbeya Tanzania and Kisumu Kenya.

we partnered with FLE [Foundation for Learning Equality], OLE [Open Learning Exchange], MIT TATA Center, MIT Ideas Global Challenge, and Rotary International, to host resources such as Khan Academy and Wikipedia on raspberry pis to allow rural locations with no internet or power to access the multitude of available resources available here. 1room hopes to be a cheap alternative to government schools that creates effective learning hubs for underserved populations in developing countries. Underserved populations include adult learners, the poor, and populations residing in rural areas. as chief technology officer I led a small team of engineers at MIT to develop both hardware and software used at our pilot sites.
